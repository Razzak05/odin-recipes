Chicken Tandoori Recipe

Chicken Tandoori is a popular and flavorful Indian dish that has gained international acclaim for its unique blend of spices and distinctive cooking method. Originating from the Indian subcontinent, this dish involves marinating chicken pieces in a vibrant mixture of yogurt and a medley of spices such as cumin, coriander, turmeric, and garam masala. The marinated chicken is then traditionally cooked in a tandoor, a clay oven that imparts a smoky and charred flavor to the meat. The result is tender, succulent chicken with a tantalizing combination of spices that create a rich and aromatic profile. The vibrant red hue of Chicken Tandoori comes from the use of ingredients like red chili powder or Kashmiri red chili, adding both color and a mild heat to the dish. Served with cooling yogurt-based sauces and accompanied by naan or rice, Chicken Tandoori is a culinary delight that showcases the diverse and bold flavors of Indian cuisine.

The preparation of Chicken Tandoori involves a meticulous process of marination, allowing the spices to infuse into the meat for an extended period. This not only imparts a depth of flavor but also tenderizes the chicken, resulting in a dish that is both aromatic and juicy. The cooking method in a tandoor adds a unique touch, creating a slightly smoky and charred exterior while maintaining the succulence of the chicken within. Often garnished with fresh cilantro and served with lemon wedges, Chicken Tandoori is a visually appealing dish that caters to both the senses of taste and smell. Whether enjoyed in an Indian restaurant or prepared at home, this iconic dish continues to be a favorite among food enthusiasts seeking a delicious and aromatic journey into the world of Indian culinary traditions.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 medium lemon, juiced
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 ¼ cups plain yogurt
  • ½ medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 2 teaspoons garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon grated fresh ginger root
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 teaspoon red food coloring (Optional)
  • 1 teaspoon yellow food coloring (Optional)
  • 2 teaspoons finely chopped cilantro
  • 1 medium lemon, cut into wedges

Steps Involved

  1. Prepare the Marinade:

    2. In a large mixing bowl, combine yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, cumin powder, coriander powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, red chili powder, Kashmiri red chili powder, salt, and vegetable oil. Mix well to form a smooth marinade.

  2. Marinate the Chicken:

    3. Clean and pat dry the chicken pieces. Make shallow cuts on the chicken to allow the marinade to penetrate. Coat the chicken thoroughly with the prepared marinade, ensuring it reaches all parts, and cover the bowl. Marinate in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours or preferably overnight for maximum flavor.

  3. Preheat the Oven or Grill:

    4. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C) or prepare a grill.

  4. Skewering or Placing on Rack:

    5. f using skewers, thread the marinated chicken onto them. If not, place the chicken pieces on a rack over a baking sheet to allow for even cooking.

  5. Cooking in the Oven or Grill:
    • Oven: Place the skewers or rack in the preheated oven and bake for 25-30 minutes or until the chicken is fully cooked and has a charred appearance.
    • Grill: If using a grill, cook the chicken over medium-high heat, turning occasionally, until fully cooked and charred on the edges.
    • Serve:Garnish the Chicken Tandoori with fresh cilantro and serve hot with lemon wedges. You can also serve it with naan or rice and yogurt-based sauces like raita.
